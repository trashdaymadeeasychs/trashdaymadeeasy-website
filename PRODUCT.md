# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **Multifamily property managers** evaluating a valet trash vendor for their building portfolio — time-pressed, operations-focused, buying on reliability and accountability
- **Build-to-rent portfolio operators** managing scattered single-family homes who need one vendor across dozens of addresses
- **STR hosts and operators** in Charleston and Atlanta who need turnover-day waste handled without coordinating between cleaners and collectors
- **HOA boards** fielding resident complaints about bins left at the curb; buying compliance and peace of mind
- **Residential homeowners** in the Charleston metro — busy families, seniors with mobility limits, frequent travelers — who want one less recurring task
- **Home builders** evaluating valet trash as a NOI-generating amenity in new construction communities

All audiences arrive with a specific problem they need solved, not a general interest in waste services. Copy and hierarchy should meet them at their problem, not educate them about the industry.

## Product Purpose

Trash Day Made Easy provides Backdoor-to-Curb valet trash and curbside bin management for residential and commercial properties in Charleston SC and Atlanta GA. The service exists to eliminate a recurring, low-stakes chore that nonetheless causes real friction — missed pickups, HOA violations, guest complaints, and resident dissatisfaction.

Success looks like: a prospect landing on the right audience page, immediately recognizing their situation, and converting within a single visit.

## Brand Personality

Premium, white-glove, effortless.

Voice: confident and direct, never salesy. Speaks to the outcome (bins handled, problem gone, never think about it again), not the process. Short sentences. No jargon.

Tone is warm but professional — like a reliable local service business that's been around long enough to not need to oversell.

## Design Principles

1. **Meet them at their problem.** Every page leads with the user's specific pain, not a generic service description. The reader should feel seen in the first two sentences.
2. **Outcome-first hierarchy.** Lead with what life looks like after the service, not how the service works. Process details belong below the fold.
3. **Earn trust through specificity.** Photo verification, exact service areas, named neighborhoods, real reviews — concrete details build more trust than adjective-heavy copy.
4. **Premium through restraint.** White-glove feel comes from spacing, typography, and confident copy — not from decorative complexity. Every design element should pass the "would a premium local service have this?" test.
5. **One clear next step.** Each page ends with a single primary CTA. No competing actions, no modal traps, no decision paralysis.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Known user segment includes seniors with mobility limitations (residential page) — ensure touch targets, font sizes, and contrast are generous. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
