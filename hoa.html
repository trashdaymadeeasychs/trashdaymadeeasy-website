The HOA Waste Challenge

One Standard for Every Home

Trash and recycling compliance is one of the most consistent sources of resident disputes in HOA communities. Bins left at the curb too long, trash set out on the wrong day, overflowing bins visible from the street — these are the complaints that end up in front of your board.

Rather than enforcing rules reactively, more HOAs are turning to valet trash service as a proactive solution — one that solves the problem before complaints happen, adds genuine value as a community amenity, and creates a consistent standard across every property in the neighborhood.

We work with HOA boards and property management companies to build community-level service agreements that cover every home and deliver a consistent result.

📋

Common HOA Waste Issues We Solve

  • Bins left at the curb for 1–2 days after pickup
  • Resident disputes over trash placement and timing
  • Inconsistent compliance with community trash rules

  • Board enforcement burden and neighbor complaints

Common Questions

HOA Service Questions Answered

Can the HOA offer valet trash as an optional add-on or must it be community-wide?
We can structure service either way. Some HOAs offer valet trash as a community-wide amenity built into HOA dues. Others offer it as an opt-in add-on that individual homeowners can choose to subscribe to. We'll work with your board to determine the best structure for your community.
How is pricing structured for HOA communities?
Pricing is based on the number of homes in the community, service frequency, and the type of service (valet trash vs curbside management). We provide community-level pricing that the HOA can either absorb into dues or pass through to homeowners. Contact us for a custom quote based on your community size.
What if some homeowners don't want the service?
For community-wide agreements, all homes are included regardless of individual preference — similar to other community services. For opt-in structures, we only service homes that have enrolled. Your board determines which approach makes the most sense for your community rules and governance structure.
Who does the HOA contact to manage the service relationship?
The HOA board or property management company works directly with our team for service setup, billing, and any issues. Individual residents can reach out to us directly for service questions. We handle communication at both levels so your board doesn't become the middleman for every resident question.
How quickly can community-wide service be set up?
Most HOA communities can be onboarded within 1–2 weeks of signing a service agreement. We build the route, coordinate with your board, and help communicate the new service to residents before the first collection date. We aim to make the launch smooth and visible as a community improvement from day one.
